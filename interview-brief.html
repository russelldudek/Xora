VP of AI thesis brief

Make every correction explainable, bounded, validated, and reusable.

Russell Dudek · Candidate thesis for 4MP

1. The real mandate

Build the learning and decision architecture that can interpret manufacturing intent, reconstruct physical machine state, explain deviation, propose a safe correction, prove the result, and decide what knowledge may responsibly generalize across machines, tools, materials, parts, programs, and environments.

2. Central operating contradictions

TensionOperating implication
Seed-stage speed vs. machine authorityMove quickly in simulation, replay, and shadow mode; require explicit evidence before expanding authority.
AI reasoning vs. physics truthDefine which layer owns estimation, explanation, constraint, correction, and verification.
Reusable platform vs. local machine realityPreserve context and scope so local fixes do not masquerade as universal intelligence.
Hands-on building vs. organizational leadershipThe VP must personally shape architecture and prototypes while building a team that can surpass the founder-stage implementation.
Operator variability vs. manufacturing intentTreat G-code, CAM, setup, fixtures, tribal knowledge, and operator decisions as evidence—not noise to average away.

3. Autonomous Correction Kernel

  1. Parse intent: geometry, tolerances, G-code, CAM context, operation sequence, and operator meaning.
  2. Reconstruct state: machine, tool, part, fixture, environment, telemetry, sensing, and metrology.
  3. Explain residual: measured facts, cause hypotheses, uncertainty, confidence, and competing explanations.
  4. Bound action: correction locus, magnitude, authority, hold conditions, fallback, and rollback.
  5. Prove result: simulation, replay, shadow inference, machine test, and post-action measurement.
  6. Admit to memory: retain only learning with explicit scope, evidence, provenance, and version.

4. Architecture questions

  • What is the canonical correction object and how is it versioned?
  • Where does manufacturing intent live when G-code is incomplete or stylistically inconsistent?
  • Which corrections are model-generated, physics-derived, rule-bound, or operator-specified?
  • How are coordinate frames, machine state, tool state, material state, and environmental state reconciled?
  • What evidence is required to progress from recommendation to bounded machine authority?
  • How is learning scoped so that portability is earned rather than assumed?
  • What simulator, replay, shadow, regression, and rollback environments exist or must be built?

5. Evidence map

6. Hard objection

The role specification is written for a deeply current production AI/ML architect with direct CNC, controls, simulation, and team-building depth. My case is not that these requirements are unimportant. My case is that I bring an uncommon integration profile across physical machines, intelligent systems, telemetry, manufacturing, quality, operations, and organizational mechanisms—and I am willing to make my technical judgment visible through a working architecture session.
