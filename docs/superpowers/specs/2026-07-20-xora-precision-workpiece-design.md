# Xora / 4MP VP of AI — Precision Workpiece Redesign

Date: 2026-07-20  
Status: Approved design direction; implementation pending  
Campaign state: Building

## 1. Purpose

Rebuild the 4MP VP of AI candidate campaign as an exceptionally composed 2026 digital experience that demonstrates how Russell Dudek would make autonomous machine correction explainable, bounded, validated, and reusable.

The redesign must replace the current generic card-stack presentation, empty evidence surfaces, redundant document controls, and weak résumé composition with one coherent employer-specific visual system spanning the website, résumé, cover letter, interview brief, 120-day plan, Correction Qualification Record, and generated PDFs.

The campaign remains clearly identified as an independent candidate work product. It must not imply 4MP or Xora endorsement or claim knowledge of undisclosed internal systems.

## 2. Approved Creative Direction

### Precision Workpiece

The campaign is organized around a sculptural precision workpiece undergoing five visible state changes:

1. measured against manufacturing intent;
2. diagnosed through a residual field;
3. constrained by physics, geometry, evidence, and authority;
4. corrected through a bounded intervention;
5. qualified for local reuse, broader learning, shadow continuation, or hold.

This is not a decorative machining motif. It is the visual expression of the candidate thesis and the role's real operating problem.

### Thesis

**Make every correction explainable, bounded, validated, and reusable.**

### Real mandate

Build the intelligence and operating architecture that converts CNC manufacturing intent, physical machine state, geometry, metrology evidence, physics constraints, and production feedback into safe corrective action—and build the seed-stage AI organization capable of making that system compound.

## 3. Design Principles

The experience must feel:

- precise rather than sterile;
- technically authoritative rather than dashboard-like;
- premium and editorial rather than template-driven;
- industrial without relying on generic factory photography;
- dynamic only where motion improves causal understanding;
- restrained enough that the résumé and application documents remain credible executive artifacts.

The full page must be art-directed. Later sections may not collapse into repeated interchangeable cards.

### Required brand package

## 5. Visitor Journey

### 5.1 Orient

The opening scene identifies 4MP, the VP of AI role, Russell, and the actual mandate without asking for a download or meeting.

### 5.2 Give value

Before any application CTA dominates, the visitor receives:

- the central tension: digital intent can be precise while physical reality remains variable;
- the candidate thesis;
- an immediately useful visual model showing how a residual becomes—or does not become—an authorized correction.

### 5.3 Invite judgment

A low-friction scenario control lets the visitor choose:

- thermal drift;
- tool wear;
- fixture shift;
- program ambiguity.

The selected scenario changes consequential outputs, not merely colors or explanatory copy.

### 5.4 Establish evidence

The experience then shows why Russell's verified background transfers to this mandate through a career-to-capability trace rather than six isolated cards.

### 5.5 Show entry path

The 120-day plan appears as four calibrated passes that build from one qualified correction class toward a repeatable platform and organization.

### 5.6 Offer application materials

Only after the thesis, operating model, and evidence are established does the site present the application dossier.

## 6. Information Architecture

### 6.1 Navigation

Use a restrained sticky navigation with real section orientation:

- Thesis;
- Workpiece;
- Evidence;
- 120 Days;
- Dossier.

A compact active-section indicator may be used only when tied to the visitor's actual scroll position. No fabricated completion percentage.

### 6.2 Hero: The Qualified Workpiece

The hero is an asymmetric editorial composition.

Left side:

- official 4MP identity lockup;
- independent candidate qualifier;
- thesis headline;
- one concise explanation of the real mandate;
- one primary action: `Explore the correction`; no résumé CTA above the initial insight.

Right side:

- original precision-workpiece rendering;
- nominal geometry;
- observed residual field;
- datum and coordinate marks;
- qualification envelope;
- correction vectors;
- a final disposition seal.

The workpiece must remain visually legible as a composed object, not an abstract graph or generic glowing network.

### 6.3 Tension Spread

Replace the existing four-card mandate grid with a magazine-style technical spread showing four operating contradictions around one central proposition:

- seed-stage velocity vs machine-level consequence;
- physics structure vs production variability;
- local correction vs reusable learning;
- hands-on technical authority vs organization building.

Each tension is connected to a concrete decision the VP must make easier.

### 6.4 Autonomous Correction Kernel

The six stages remain case-specific:

`Parse intent -> Reconstruct state -> Explain residual -> Bound action -> Prove result -> Admit to memory`

They should appear as one continuous qualification path embedded into the workpiece narrative rather than six equal boxes.

Each stage has:

- a visible input;
- an output state;
- a named failure or hold condition;
- the human or system authority boundary;
- the evidence needed to proceed.

### 6.5 Interactive Scenario Review

#### Smart default

Open in `Thermal drift — illustrative baseline` with the complete output visible before interaction.

#### Controls

Use four accessible segmented controls or a compact scenario rail. Include a clearly discoverable `Reset baseline` action.

#### Scenario-dependent state

Each selection must change:

- workpiece residual shape or heat/contour distribution;
- cause hypothesis;
- correction locus;
- authority posture;
- minimum proof;
- learning scope;
- disposition;
- the central workpiece label;
- the final qualification boundary.

#### Dispositions

Use semantically distinct final states:

- bounded correction;
- qualified adjustment;
- confirm registration;
- human hold required.

Do not imply these are 4MP's internal production states. Label the model as illustrative candidate reasoning.

Each career record must visibly include:

No content may depend on inherited text color. Every text/background combination must pass contrast checks.

### 6.7 120-Day Calibrated Passes

Present the entry plan as four passes across one horizontal or vertical inspection record:

- Days 0–30: Instrument the problem;
- Days 31–60: Close a narrow loop;
- Days 61–90: Stress the boundary;
- Days 91–120: Install the compounding system.

Each pass includes:

- the operating question;
- principal work;
- exit evidence;
- the resulting authority level.

The layout must recompose for tablet and mobile rather than merely stack desktop cards.

### 6.8 Application Dossier

Remove the duplicate download-button rail.

1. Role-aligned résumé;
2. Cover letter;
3. Interview thesis brief.

Use two supporting operating artifacts:

4. 120-day entry plan;
5. Correction Qualification Record.

Each surface contains:

- one native `Download PDF` action inside the route itself.

The dossier section itself must not duplicate download actions already provided on document routes.

### 6.9 Closing

The final section should invite one credible next action:

`Review the role-aligned résumé`

Secondary text may provide Russell's direct contact information. Do not ask for a meeting before the argument has been delivered.

## 7. Motion System

### 7.1 Signature sequence

The workpiece animation runs once on first meaningful entry or manual replay:

1. nominal geometry appears;
2. measurement sweep reveals residual;
3. the cause region isolates;
4. correction vectors resolve inside the qualification envelope;
5. post-correction geometry settles;
6. the final disposition and learning scope lock into place.

The animation must reach a stable, fully readable final state. No endless moving dots, decorative orbiting, or perpetual scanner motion.

### 7.2 Scenario transition

Scenario selection replays only the state changes needed to teach the new case. Rapid selection must settle on the final requested scenario.

### 7.3 Reduced motion

With `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`:

- all geometry resolves immediately;
- no continuous movement remains;
- active scenario, cause, authority, proof, and disposition remain fully legible;
- focus and state changes remain clear without relying on animation.

## 8. Responsive Art Direction

### Desktop and laptop

- preserve the asymmetrical hero;
- workpiece remains large enough for labels and residual detail;
- evidence trace is not a six-card grid;
- document dossier shows hierarchy between primary and supporting artifacts.

### Tablet

- recompose the hero into stacked but overlapping editorial planes;
- move annotation labels into a dedicated inspection strip when needed;
- maintain visible causal connection between workpiece, readout, and controls.

### Mobile

- use a mobile-specific workpiece composition rather than shrinking the desktop SVG blindly;
- simplify annotation density while preserving nominal, observed, correction, proof, and disposition states;
- keep scenario controls touch-friendly and horizontally scroll-free;
- convert evidence trace into an alternating record sequence, not generic cards;
- preserve employer identity and candidate qualifier above the fold.

### Narrow phone

Validate at 320 px and 390 px widths. No internal horizontal scrolling may be required to understand the operating model.

## 12. Technical Architecture

### Source files

The implementation should retain a small, readable static architecture:

- `index.html`;
- `styles.css`;
- `brand-tokens.css`;
- `app.js`;
- document HTML routes;
- generated PDFs under `docs/`;
- official assets under `assets/brand/`;
- original campaign graphics under `assets/campaign/`;
- neutral public documentation and audit records.

### Component responsibilities

- HTML owns semantic content and meaningful default state.
- CSS owns responsive composition, visual system, print geometry, reduced-motion presentation, focus states, and contrast.
- JavaScript owns scenario state, SVG workpiece transitions, accessible labels, reset/replay behavior, and active-section orientation.
- JavaScript may not inject duplicate identity lockups, document controls, or essential narrative content.

### Scenario data contract

Each scenario record must provide:

- key and label;
- observed residual geometry or field parameters;
- cause hypothesis;
- correction locus;
- authority posture;
- proof requirement;
- learning scope;
- disposition;
- workpiece annotation labels;
- accessible summary;
- motion timing parameters only when materially different.

## 13. Accessibility

Required:

- semantic landmarks and heading order;
- skip link;
- visible keyboard focus;
- native buttons for scenario selection;
- `aria-pressed` or appropriate tab semantics;
- live-region updates that are concise and non-repetitive;
- accessible SVG title/description or equivalent text summary;
- no information conveyed by color alone;
- contrast meeting WCAG AA at minimum;
- touch targets of at least 44 px where practical;
- reduced-motion support;
- document links and downloads usable by keyboard and assistive technology.

## 14. Error and Fallback Behavior

- If JavaScript fails, the thermal baseline and full narrative remain visible.
- If the workpiece SVG cannot animate, the resolved static state remains complete.
- Missing optional imagery must not collapse layout or obscure company identity.
- PDF download links must resolve to committed files; broken links fail publication.
- Rapid scenario interaction must cancel or supersede prior transitions without leaving mixed state.

## 15. QA and Acceptance Criteria

### Visual

- no empty evidence surfaces;
- no white text on white cards or equivalent contrast defect;
- no generic repeated card stack as the primary full-page structure;
- no redundant document download rail;
- no unfinished later sections;
- live visual review must feel materially more authored and premium than the rejected version.

### Interaction

For all four scenarios verify:

- selected control state;
- changed workpiece geometry or residual field;
- changed cause, correction locus, authority, proof, learning scope, and disposition;
- correct final annotation and accessibility summary;
- stable final state after animation;
- reset baseline;
- keyboard operation;
- rapid-selection correctness;
- reduced-motion behavior.

### Responsive

Render and inspect at minimum:

- 1440 × 900;
- 1280 × 800;
- 768 × 1024;
- 390 × 844;
- 320 px narrow phone;
- reduced-motion mode.

Verify:

- no horizontal overflow;
- no annotation/text collision;
- no sticky-header coverage;
- no workpiece labels clipped outside the viewport;
- no internal scroll required to understand the artifact;
- employer identity remains visible and correctly proportioned.

### Documents

- résumé PDF exactly two pages;
- cover-letter PDF exactly one page;
- all other PDFs generated and visually reviewed;
- no microscopic type;
- no clipped or split modules;
- page-one résumé balance passes;
- reciprocal résumé/cover-letter navigation works;
- one native `Download PDF` action per document route;
- PDF links open the committed same-origin files.

### Evidence and confidentiality

- no invented titles, metrics, CNC tenure, production-model ownership, or advanced degree;
- no confidential clients;
- no private campaign-system name or source-repository invitation in public files or PDF metadata;
- full candidate contact block uses verified values;
- complete visible candidate-vision URL appears in résumé and cover letter.

### Publication

The campaign cannot be marked complete until the complete source, brand package, assets, HTML routes, generated PDFs, and neutral documentation are verified on `main`, and the live deployment corresponds to the audited source.
